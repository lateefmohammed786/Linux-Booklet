# üü• Disadvantages of Linux

## ‚ùå 1. Limited Support for Some Applications  
Certain commercial software isn't natively available, including:  
- Adobe Photoshop  
- Microsoft Office (full desktop version)  
- Some industry-specific applications  

Workarounds exist, but not always perfect.

---

## ‚ùå 2. Learning Curve  
New users may find Linux challenging due to:  
- Command-line usage  
- Permissions model  
- Different filesystem structure  

However, it becomes easy with practice.

---

## ‚ùå 3. Gaming Support  
Although improving, Linux still has:  
- Limited native game availability  
- Occasional driver or compatibility issues  

Steam Proton and Wine help, but not 100%.

---

## ‚ùå 4. Hardware Compatibility Issues  
Some drivers (especially Wi-Fi, printers, older GPUs) may require manual installation or may not be fully supported.

---

## ‚ùå 5. Fragmentation  
There are hundreds of Linux distributions.  
While choice is good, it may overwhelm beginners.

---

## ‚ùå 6. Paid Enterprise Support  
Community support is free, but enterprise-grade support (e.g., RHEL, SUSE) may require subscriptions.
